Overview

In the world of tennis there are four major tournaments. The four tournaments are the Australian Open which is held in January, the French Open in late May, Wimbledon in late June, and the US Open which is held in August-Septmember. These tournaments offer the most prize money, most ranking points, and the most public and media attention. The four tournaments also show a variety of playing surfaces as hard, clay, and grass courts can be seen. The tournaments also allow players to achieve the "Grand Slam". A Grand Slam is when a player wins all four tournaments in a calender year.

Australian Open

The Australian Open is played in the middle of each January in Melbourne, Australia. The tourament runs for two weeks and is played by both mens and womens players and has both singles and doubles tournaments. Novak Djokovic is the all time leader in tournament titles with nine for men and Margaret Court with 11 for womens.. The first Australian Open was held in 1905 and since the beginning there have been 110 Australian Open tournaments. From 1905-1988 the playing surface was grass and was switched to a hard surface in 1988 and has remained that way ever since.

Australian Open Winners

Novak Djokovic has nine Australian Open singles titles which is the most by any male player. Adrian Quist won 10 Australian Open doubles throughout his career and to this day is the most by any male player. Margaret Court won the singles tournament 11 times which is enough for her to lead all women. Thelma Coyne Long owns the most doubles wins in the womens doubles tournament. The current champions are Rafael Nadal for mens singles, Nick Kyrigos and Thanasi Kokkinakis for mens doubles, Ashleigh Barty for womens singles, and Barbora Krejcikova and Katerina Siniakova for womens doubles. The 2023 Australian Open will be played from January 16- January 29.

French Open

The French Open is a two week tourament held at the Stade Roland Garros in Paris, France. The tournament is played on a clay court and is the only major tournament to be played on clay. It is the second of the major tournaments to be played each year, following up the Australian Open. Due to the clay surface, the French Open is widely regarded as the most physically demanding tennis tournament in the world. The first French Open was played in 1891 and has been played 126 times since then. The French Open has served as a Grand Slam event since 1925 and has been play 92 times since then. The playing surface has been clay since 1908 but from 1891-1908 the playing surface was sand like.

French Open Winners

Rafael Nadal has won the most mens singles titles at the French Open by winning 14 times. Roy Emerson won the doubles tourament six times at the French Open and that is good enough to be the most mens doubles titles of all time. On the womens side Chris Evert has the most singles titles with seven and Martina Navratilova has the crown with seven womens doubles titles.

Wimbledon

The Wimbledon Championships or just Wimbledon is the world's oldest tennis tournament and if regarded as the most prestigious. The tournament is held in late June and into July at the All England Club in Wimbledon, London. Wimbledon is the only major tourament played on grass, which is the traditional playing surface. The first Wimbledon tournament was held in 1877 and there have been 135 editions since then. The prize money at the 2022 tournament was about $42.5 million. Roger Federer and Martina Navratilova are the mens and womens all time leader in Wimbledon titles. Federer has 8 titles and Navratilova has 9. Traditions at Wimbledon include an all white dress code for players and unlike other tournaments, advertising is kept to a minimum.

Wimbelon Winners

Novak Djokovic is the current winner at Wimbledon for mens at Roger Federer has most mens wins all time. The current winners in doubles are Matthew Edben and Max Purcell. Todd Woodbridge had nine doubles wins at Wimbledon during his career and that is enough to own the most for men all time.

US Open

The US Open is the fourth and last Grand Slam tournamnet to be played each year. The tournament is played on a hard court and is played in Queens, New York. The US Open begins on the last Monday of August and lasts for two weeks. The tourament was founded in 1881 and has been played 142 times in its history. Throughout the history of the US Open, the tournament has been played hard, clay, and grass surfaces. They played on grass from 1881-1974, clay from 1975-1977, and on a hard surface since 1978.

US Open Winners

There is a three-way tie for most singles titles on the mens side of thing. Richard Sears, William Larned, and Bill Tilden all have won the US Open seven times. There is also a three-way tie for the most mens doubles wins at the US Open. Mike Bryan, Richard Sears, and Holcombe Ward each have won the doubles tourament six times. Molla Mallory won the womens singles tourament eight times, which is most my a women all time. Margaret Osborn dupont has won a record 13 womens doubles tournament at the US Open. The current champions are Carlos Alcaraz in mens singles and Rajeev Ram and Joe Salisbury in the mens doubles. On the womens side, Iga Swiatek is the singles champion and Barbora Krejcikova and Katerina Siniakova are the doubles winners.
